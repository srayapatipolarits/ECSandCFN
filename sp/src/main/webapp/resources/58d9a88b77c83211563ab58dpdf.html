Primary personality: Navigator

Summary

Individuals with a NAVIGATOR personality portrait tend to exhibit seemingly opposing strengths. As a NAVIGATOR, Viru may not always be fully aware of how his strong personality is perceived by others. He desires tangible results and perfection.

Viru can be both aggressive and sensitive at the same time in his quest to achieve his desired outcome. Decision making can be stalled by his desire to explore all possible options before arriving at a conclusive decision. With a strong task orientation, he is likely to implement sound changes and has the ability to plan considerably.

Though he may not realize it, Viru can sometimes prioritize the “mission” over the personal relationships involved. As such, personal relationships may not receive the attention necessary to keep a team on the same page, especially when changes are implemented.

Viru is inclined to desire freedom to explore and re-check decisions, and may project an aloofness or bluntness that creates distance from work colleagues.

EXTERNAL ASPECTS

JOB MATCH

Since the NAVIGATOR personality seeks to be logical, accurate, practical and thorough, Viru may function most effectively when the work environment is structured and organized. He works at his best when given independence and presented with problems and challenges to tackle. In the job description, he prefers the opportunity to: analyze, be accurate and in control, build and create; critique and improve, decide, develop, direct, initiate solutions and manage; organize, plan, process, perform quality control and research facts; solve problems, work with objects, work with data, supervise and think things through.

Communicating With Viru

Since NAVIGATORS are motivated by logic and not emotion, Viru resists change unless he understands the reasons behind it. Provide well-researched details, facts and data to explain the required change. Paint the “big picture” before providing explanations.

Disagreeing With Viru

Carefully document facts and data that have been well researched. Offer proof before asking him to consider the case. Do not push too hard for an immediate decision. Allow time to consider the evidence, apply logic and process the information. Look for areas of agreement to serve as a foundation for change.

Negative Perceptions Viru May Have Of Others

He may judge others as being disorganized, less prepared, less accurate and unproductive. He is likely to feel offended by or lose respect for others who publicly criticize, embarrass or correct him.

Negative Perceptions Others May Have Of Viru

Others may see Viru as very territorial. Conflict may occur over who is in control and who is correct. He may be seen as picky or pushy with a “know-it-all” attitude. Others may see him as overbearing, demanding or unyielding.

Notable Characteristics

Viru is a self-starter who responds immediately to competition in an effort to avoid unnecessary risks or trouble. He can be logical and critical, employing a personal approach to attain a goal. Problems are seen as a challenge, requiring unique and analytical efforts. At times, Viru can be aggressive, systematic, factual and impatient. He may pick up little nuances and hidden meanings that are often missed by others.

FUNDAMENTAL DESIRES/DRIVE

Viru enjoys discovering new possibilities given the time to ponder, explore and probe. In fact, he often uncovers more alternatives than work associates can imagine.

RECOMMENDED IMPROVEMENTS

Viru tends to be carefully aggressive, tensely tactful, self-critical and overly serious. His high standards and critical nature may be problematic. There is a tension between the “big picture” and getting the details together that may leave him struggling with indecision. The impact this has on others is stronger than he may realize. He either empower others with his strong personality or leaves them feeling hurt and criticized.

HOW TO SUPERVISE AND ENCOURAGE

Viru needs a manager who will tap into his creative flow and put his better ideas into practice. He needs a leader who will help get things finished and initiate new projects. It is important to have a manager who is direct and with whom he can be direct. A passive or carefree manager is not going to work. He needs challenging assignments that will be stimulating and a manager who reminds him to soften his approach.

Personality under pressure: Visionary

Summary

Individuals with a VISIONARY personality portrait exhibit some of the strongest personality characteristics. As a VISIONARY, Viru may not always be fully aware of the impact his strong personality may have on work associates and others. He is typically a strong individualist who continually strives to seek new objectives and agendas.

In a work environment, others may see Viru as being on a mission. He is often forward-thinking in his approach, considering the next project before the current one is even completed. Being extremely independent and self-reliant, he may prefer to find his own solution to an obstacle and be relatively free from group constraints. Innovative solutions are often discovered because of his internal belief that there must be a better way.

Viru may sometimes use a direct and forceful approach to get things accomplished, resorting to manipulation to accomplish objectives and doing what it takes to overcome obstacles. He may also become belligerent when required to participate in group situations that are not conducive to an individualistic approach. In fact, some people may be intimidated by his tenacity and high expectations. Viru has the capacity for the more difficult endeavors that are unsuitable for softer personalities.

Viru is typically interested in achieving goals, thriving on challenges and making important advancements. Since he is focused on results, he could leave behind people who do not possess the same level of stamina and endurance. A lack of empathy may be expressed with an attitude of “just get over it”.

JOB MATCH

The VISIONARY is a “take charge” kind of person, so Viru may function most effectively when given independence, challenges, obstacles to overcome, problems to solve and minimal details. In the job description, He prefers the opportunity to: be in control, build, create, decide, develop and direct; initiate solutions, lead others, manage, solve problems and supervise.

Communicating With Viru

Provide the “big picture” before providing any explanations, details and concerns. Since VISIONARIES are not good listeners by nature, compose thoughts into a short memo for Viru that details the problems, options and recommended actions. Otherwise there’s the danger of losing his attention with the details.

Disagreeing With Viru

First, find the larger goal that can be agreed upon, then propose the plan that will expedite the plan. He can be highly committed to reaching a goal, with the particular methodology a lesser priority. Pointing out small and insignificant shortcomings will erode the relationship over time. Be willing to discuss matters openly in a democratic manner, but avoid asking “why”. Instead, make inquiries by asking, “How did you arrive at your decision?” Or “I’m not sure if I understand, can you tell me more?” Taking this approach during conflict will minimize his defensiveness. Being respectfully direct and allowing frankness will go a long way. When making a request, let Viru know what you want, then give him the latitude to accomplish the request in his own way.

Negative Perceptions Viru May Have Of Others

He may see others with a similar personality as territorial. Conflict may ensue over control. He may be turned off by “too much talk” and “not enough action”. He may see some people as being less motivated or trying to stall the process with trivial or excessive details.

Negative Perceptions Others May Have Of Viru

Others may see him as disinterested or “on a mission”. Those with soft personalities may see him as insensitive, self-centered or stubborn. Detailed people may perceive him as having a “know-it-all” attitude.

Notable Characteristics

Viru is generally assertive, action-oriented and very independent. With a flair for adventure, he is typically determined and not easily intimidated. Being self-motivated and determined, he thrives on solving difficult problems with brain power and tenacity.

Viru possesses a hard, goal-driven orientation. He tends to charge ahead against all resistance and obstacles. Fear is not a common emotion as he seems to thrive on challenges and attack them with intense determination. The only thing he fears is loss of control or freedom to achieve the desired results.

Viru’s impact on others is stronger than he may realize. With his strong personality, Viru either empowers people or leaves them feeling hurt and criticized. At times, he may be too blunt and critical. When someone doesn't measure up to his standards, Viru can be prone to hurting their feelings. Learning to delegate, communicate softly and be patient would be helpful.

Viru can be incredibly productive with the right manager. He works best with a manager who is direct and straightforward, and with whom he can be direct. He wants to feel free to raise issues and negotiate on equal ground. Projects that may intimidate others can challenge Viru. When told that something is impossible, Viru is inclined to find a solution. A manager may have to remind him of just how potent his comments may be and make him aware of the impact words may have on work associates and subordinates.

INTUITIVELY vs. CONCRETELY

At any given time a person is either taking in information or making decisions based upon information already received. Using concrete or intuitive data are two ways of perceiving information. The concrete processor prefers to utilize what can be actually seen, heard, touched, tasted, or smelled, rather than counting on the possibilities of what could be. The intuitive processor prefers to gather information by application and thinking through as many different scenarios as possible.

AFFECTIVE vs. COGNITIVE

People use both their head and heart in making decisions but typically prefer and are better at one over the other. The person that prefers the head (cognitive) function tends to make decisions based on what seems to be logical with objective information. This does not mean that the individual never makes decisions based on his heart. A person that prefers the heart (affective) function in making decisions is likely to be more person-centered and/or value-centered. This does not mean that this person is overly emotional or illogical. It is not suggesting that the individual will never make a decision based on his head.

Fundamental Needs

There are three fundamental needs that everyone experiences in life. However, one will usually dominate and contribute significantly to a person's outlook, attitude, motivation, and behavior.

Control (30%)Security (33%)Significance (37%)

30% CONTROL

Control manifests itself in efforts to influence or manage circumstances or people. It carries a longing for a setting that will allow the opportunity to control the work setting or have the power to manage people/events.

37% SIGNIFICANCE

Significance comes by way of approval or affection from significant others. It brings a desire for a career setting that has a positive effect on others. This usually comes about through cooperative efforts or association with a particular group of people.

33% SECURITY

Security comes by way of acquisition of assets, position, education, or a sense of belonging. Those motivated by security are likely attracted to a career setting that provides for a stable future.

Principles that apply to
Fundamental Needs

  • One of the three Fundamental Needs will be dominant throughout most of a person's life, but many people will identify a Secondary Need as well.
  • Fundamental Needs have a significant impact on one's attitude, behavior, and motivation in the work context.
  • A mismanaged Fundamental Need can become a destructive force in any area of life.
  • Setbacks may cause a temporary shift away from the Fundamental Need to the Secondary Need.
  • Identifying a Fundamental Need can increase the understanding of a person's attitudes, motivation, and behavior.

OUTWARD PROCESSOR vs. INWARD PROCESSOR

A 50/50 result indicates that a person's decision making varies based on the particular situation.

17% OUTWARD PROCESSOR

Outward Processors often utilize interaction with others as a way to process information and arrive at a wise decision. They have a need to communicate their thoughts openly in order to discern which ideas best contribute to a wise decision. It is this interaction with others that allows them to convert an idea to a decision.

Others may think this type of person thinks out loud, allowing others to hear both their good and bad ideas. Some may get the impression that Outward Processors figure out what they think as they speak. Observers may not fully understand the need for engaging conversation in order for the Outward Processor to organize and clarify the thinking process.

83% INWARD PROCESSOR

Inward Processors usually keep their thoughts private until they are convinced that the ideas are sound. They sort through their thoughts privately, and discern which ideas best contribute to a wise decision. It is in their private world that they can solidify their decisions.

Others may think of this kind of person as one who holds their cards close or even as uptight about sharing thoughts. Some may get the impression the Inward Processor is unwilling to take the risk of sharing all their ideas. Inward Processors may be accused of not being open to the exchange of ideas, failing to fully understand their need for privacy in order to organize and clarify the thought process.

CAREFUL DECIDER vs. RAPID DECIDER

A 50/50 result indicates that a person's decision making varies based on the particular situation.

67% CAREFUL DECIDER

Careful Deciders usually have to investigate all the options before making a decision, wanting to be certain of the outcome. They tend to go step by step with a methodical style of processing information and arriving at a decision.

Others may see this kind of person as bogging down the process or as being overly concerned with small matters.

33% RAPID DECIDER

Rapid Deciders have a quick sense of what will work and are able to intuitively process information and arrive at a decision without the need to go step by step.

Others may see this kind of person as rushing to judgment, carelessly making decisions, which may or may not be the case.
