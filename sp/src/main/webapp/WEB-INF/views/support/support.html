Dashboard

The Dashboard & News Feed

The Dashboard & Newsfeed is a social epicenter that places personalized learning and team interaction side by side to create a unified social learning experience. The Dashboard in the left column connects to Prism Portrait, goals, programs and relationships, as well as intelligent content recommendations from over 70 categories of real skills. The News Feed in the right column connects you to your organizational culture and social communities with team member posts, polls, content, comments, activities, and company announcements.

Universal Tools

The orange toolbar on the bottom of the SurePeople platform offers easy access to a collection of tools and features that are most valuable for use throughout the platform, including:

Get Started Tutorial - guides you through your Prism Portrait, Prism Lens, Relationship Advisor, and ERT-I Development.

The Action Plan - is a centralized location for all your personalized learning. Program steps, ERT-I Development content, and Prism Lens Requests populate the Action Plan to keep you on top of any needed action across the platform.

Networks - are groups that are either created by you or another platform member in your organization. Use this space to collaborate with your teams, and groups or give updates on projects and happenings.

Notes - is your personal journal for keeping track of anything that’s worth keeping track of.

Bookmarks - are your personal list of favorite content found in the Knowledge Center.

Member Details - gives you access to your personal profile and account details.

Support - gives you access to SurePeople’s Client Solutions for answering platform and account questions, or for trouble shooting through issues.

Emotional Relational & Team Intelligence (ERT-i™)

Prism® Portrait

The SurePeople Prism®

The SurePeople Prism® is the most comprehensive psychometric algorithm in the human analytics industry. It measures an individual's emotional, relational and team intelligence (ERT-i™) by analyzing more than 50 unique traits based on the seven key attributes for uncovering ERT-i™ insights: personality (primary & under pressure), motivation, learning style, decision making, conflict management, fundamental needs and processing blueprint.
Play Video

Reliability & Validity of the Prism®

The SurePeople Prism® has exceeded all standards established for psychometric assessments, including: internal consistency and reliability, construct validity, and test-retest validity and reliability.

Proven with scientific reliability, validity and decades of proprietary research across 47 nationalities with 1,200 initial test subjects, the SurePeople Prism® is built on a set of testing standards collectively developed by the American Educational Research Association (AERA), American Psychological Association (APA) and National Council on Measurement in Education (NCME).

To request more information on SurePeople's scientific reliability and validity, please email Prism@surepeople.com.

Disagreeing With Prism® Results

Disagreeing with your results creates a great opportunity for you to discuss them with peers who know you best. By engaging others in conversation about your results you may uncover opportunities for growth and understanding.

Prism Lens™

The Prism Lens™

The Prism Lens™ is a powerful way to learn how others perceive you. How you're perceived is significant for growing your emotional, relational and team intelligence (ERT-i™), and reflects how others see your personal brand.

Invite friends and colleagues to create a Lens™ on your behalf, and they'll be asked a series of questions that will yield a summary of your personality type through their eyes. This analysis of your notable characteristics will reveal increasingly personalized learning pathways to grow your ERT-i™ by influencing the types of practice areas recommended for you in your development plan.
Play Video

Prism Lens™ Requests

Prism Lens™ invites can include any individual who you feel would help you gain personal and professional insight into yourself. It's recommended to invite people who know you well in order to make the most out of the Prism Lens™.

Open the main menu and select Prism Lens™. Then, simply add the name of an existing SurePeople member or any email address to initiate a Lens request. You'll be asked to provide a date that you'd like the Lens completed by. Make sure to allow the person you're requesting enough time to complete the Lens.

Troubleshooting Unreceived Prism Lens™ Requests

First, please verify that you have entered the correct email address for the individual. If you have not entered the correct email address for the individual you will need to delete their invitation under your Prism Lens requests. You may then send the individual a new invitation to take the Lens. If you have confirmed that the email address is valid, please ask the individual to check their junk mail or spam folder. If the email address is correct and the invitation is not in the individual's junk mail or spam folder, please contact SurePeople Client Solutions for further assistance.

Removing Prism Lens™ Results

To delete a Lens result simply select delete next to the specific person's name on the Lens page. Once deleted you'll need to request a new Lens if you want their perspective again.

Incomplete Lens™ Requests

If the Prism Lens™ request has not been completed by the indicated date, simply delete the request and send a new Lens invitation.

ERT-i™ Development

ERT-i™ Development

Your SurePeople recommended practice areas are determined by pulling key traits and measurements from your Prism® results. Each Prism® Portrait offers an in-depth look at the most needed growth opportunities for similar types of individuals. By cross-referencing these areas with additional measurements from the Prism Lens, we're able to create intelligent recommendations that focus on precise opportunities for further development. You can customize your ERT-i™ Development plan to include any practice area. You must always have at least one practice area selected; however, you are free to work on up to six practice areas at a time. To update your ERT-i™ Development plan, navigate to ERT-i™ Development in the main menu and then select Customize ERT-i™ on the top right portion of the page.

Ongoing Development

ERT-i™ is all about ongoing development. However, If you've completed all of the Knowledge Building articles in a practice area, simply search through the Knowledge Center for similar topics. ERT-i™ topics are relatable to one another and often times you'll find valuable information about similar topics will help you gain a better understanding of your original practice area. To view what you have already completed; simply select Completed Articles in the Knowledge Building section under the corresponding practice area.

ERT-i Content Tools

There are several ways to make content work for you on the SurePeople platform. For one, the bookmark tag beneath each article title allows you to save and view content at a later time. There's also an option to recommend an article to let others know the information worked for you. The share tag beneath each article title allows you to share valuable content with others. Use Post a Note to record some thoughts on the article and include a reference to the original content. Most notably, you can highlight any text in an article and add it to either a private note or to a group discussion.

Auto Learning

Auto Learning delivers Knowledge Building content from your selected practice areas directly to your email. To turn on or off Auto Learning emails navigate to your ERT-i™ Development from the main menu and toggle the Auto-Learning button on the left side of the page.

Learning & Development Tools

ERT-i™ Development includes Notes & Feedback. Both tools keep you accountable to developing within each Practice Area. As an element of self-awareness and understanding the Notes tool allows you to record thoughts and actions tied to Development Strategies or Practice Areas. You can also utilize the Feedback Tool to gain a greater understanding of how your development efforts are seen by others. This form of contextual feedback allows you to quickly receive constructive or complementary criticism that can help you in real time.

The Relationship Advisor allows you to perform side-by-side personality comparisons of any two individuals. Use this powerful tool to navigate relationship dynamics, coach one another on differences in work styles, and help improve communication and trust between yourself and your team members. The Relationship Advisor is easily accessible from either the bottom left corner of the main dashboard, or through the navigation menu.

Performance

Blueprint

The BluePrint

No matter what your role or responsibility it’s important to anchor yourself in a central plan before the demands and expectations of others derail you from the true purpose of your work. That’s why SurePeople created the BluePrint, an approach to performance management and project tracking. The BluePrint helps you think boldly yet realistically about the strategies and tactics that will bridge the gap between where you are now, and where you want to be.

The Four Steps Of BluePrint

The BluePrint building process has four main steps: Crafting a Mission Statement, Identifying Objectives, Identifying Key Initiatives and Setting Critical Success Measures. Get started creating your BluePrint. A Mission Statement helps define who you are and what you represent. Objectives are the strategic areas you will focus on to execute your mission. Key Initiatives are the actionable tactics that are carried out to complete the objective.

Competencies

Competencies

Competencies are the measurable skills, attitudes, or attributes that distinguish an individual's level of performance. Competencies are predetermined by your organization. This distinct set of competencies you've been assigned to, represent the behaviors needed to excel for anyone at your level within your organization. In combination with your personal ERT-i™, these role-related competencies should be used as benchmarks for growing and developing your performance. Your organization will use competencies to evaluate you on your performance. Data can be gathered from self, peer and manager evaluations on your specific competency areas.

Completing A Competency Evaluation

Once an evaluation has been initiated an email will be sent notifying you that a new evaluation period has begun. Follow the links in the email or begin your evaluation from your Action Plan. Once you activate the evaluation, an email will be sent to your manager requesting feedback on your performance and development. You'll then be prompted to begin evaluating your own performance and development.

Evaluating A Peer Or Direct Report

If you receive an email notifying you that a peer or direct report has requested you to provide an evaluation of their competencies; please take the time to reflect on the observable behaviors, skills, attitudes, or attributes that distinguish your peer or direct report’s level of performance.

Pulse Surveys

The SurePeople Pulse™

The SurePeople Pulse™ is a survey that captures employee feedback and company best practices through a safe and anonymous channel. A Pulse can be sent to the entire population or within specific groups to determine employee satisfaction and address specific needs. The pre-designed survey is used to assess your organization's health on measures of culture, engagement and leadership. Customized Pulses are used to obtain real-time feedback about a specific issue or gain insights on products and services from partners, clients or any group across the organization.

Pulse Anonymity

Pulse answers are completely anonymous. This benefits the individual and the organization allowing candid and honest answers, which will assist leadership in making genuine improvements in company procedures.

Starting a Pulse

Visit the Performance page to start a new Pulse. Select the intended Pulse from the drop down menu and indicate the end date for the survey period.

Competency Manager

The Competency Manager Tool

The Competency Manager tool allows an organization to assign role-based competency profiles to individuals or groups. These competency profiles are tailored to meet the individual's needs for understanding how organizational strategy ties to what's expected of them in their position. An administrator of Competency Manager also has the ability to initiate evaluation periods with the ability to select "self, manager, and peer review" options.

Competencies

Competencies are the measurable skills, attitudes, or attributes that distinguish an individual's level of performance. For anyone to excel in their position, they need a clear understanding of how organizational strategy ties to individual achievement. Each competency profile includes development strategies and contextual knowledge building content along with notes and feedback tools to guide continued learning.

Assigning Competency Profiles

From the Account page, the administrator can assign competency profiles to members. The administrator has the option to assign profiles to individual users or can select multiple users and groups for assignment to a particular profile.

Activating and Ending Evaluations

Evaluations begin the day that the administrator selects for the evaluation period to start and ends when the administrator designates. End dates are considered “soft” which means administrators are allowed to accept or deny delayed submissions of evaluations. The evaluation period will officially end when the administrator selects “end evaluation” from the Competency Manager. Competency profiles are delivered to all members at the same time. If you have different competency profiles for different members, they cannot be sent out separately. New competency profiles may not be assigned during an evaluation period.

Evaluation Status

The administrator can see the status of user evaluations in Performance, under Competency Manager. From the dashboard the administrator or manager can review completed evaluations as well as send out notifications to groups of people that have not yet started their evaluation. The administrator can also send individual reminders; replace an individual’s evaluating manager; select peer evaluators; or initiate an individual’s evaluation process for them.

SUREPEOPLE DEFINITIONS

Candidate- An applicant who is seeking employment with an organization

Conflict Management- Embracing the knowledge that we, as well as those with whom we interact, deal with conflict in different ways allows for more productive and harmonious work relationships. The SurePeople PRISM measures distinct conflict management styles, knowing that, while no two people are alike, we all operate on spectrum that incorporates many ways of managing conflict based on specific situations.

Decision making- Understanding our decision making styles and the styles of those with whom we interact, minimizes conflict opportunities. The SurePeople PRISM measures decision making style on four distinct but related scales. Where we fall within each scale offers insight into our thought processes and personal expectations.

Emotional Relational & Team Intelligence (ERT-i™)- A critical measurement of oneself, one's emotions and one's interactions with others.

Fundamental Needs- As human beings, we constantly make decisions and take actions to fulfill our fundamental needs. Although we may not be fully aware of or able to articulate them, our fundamental needs deeply influence our behavior. The SurePeople PRISM™ measures fundamental needs separate from other facets of the SurePeople Prism, giving more comprehensive insight into what defines and drives us as individuals. The information helps us better appreciate and respond to those with whom we interact in the workplace and beyond.

Learning style- While others press forward undaunted, others are more deliberate and concerned with protocol. The measurement of learning styles speaks to personal process-are we more engaged by big ideas or details, risk-taking or analysis? The SurePeople PRISM measures how individuals process concepts and details to reveal learning styles which is an important influence on compatibility in work relationships.

Motivation- Understanding our motivations-how and why we take certain actions-is critical to success in our personal and professional lives. In the workplace, understanding the motivations of individuals can help us manage others well and work more productively on a team or with a supervisor. The SurePeople PRISM presents separate measurements that uncover our motivations with a telling and comprehensive analysis.

Personality- While everyone exhibits dominant and complementary personality characteristics, each personality is typically a compilation of various types. Some people exhibit the same personality no matter the context, but most present a real and perceptible personality shift at certain times, especially when under stress. Appreciating these types of distinctions about ourselves and others minimizes conflict and leads to greater efficiency and effectiveness in both our professional and personal lives. The SurePeople PRISM yields comprehensive and highly relevant insights into personality-how we posture and interact with others. It measures individuals within a four-dimensional scale where the relative values of each dimension combine to form a broad portrait of one's orientation towards challenges and situations.

Processing- How do we approach the people, activities, thoughts and causes that impact our lives? What energizes us? How do we process real and perceived stimuli? Are we guided by our hearts or our heads? The SurePeople PRISM addresses internal processes, often difficult to ascertain without the proper context. The resulting measurements offer a precise view of an individual's approach to processing internal and external stimuli, information that is especially constructive in team environments.

Reliability- The extent to which an assessment yields the same results over time

SurePeople Member- Any individual who has a paid subscription to an Individual or Business Account with SurePeople products and services.

SurePeople Prism Lens™- An assessment that measures others' perceptions of an individual's emotional, relational and team intelligence

SurePeople PULSE™- A survey tool that allows an organization to gain insight into its culture, leadership and loyalty

Validity- The extent to which an assessment measures what it is supposed to measure
